	<p class="project">
		<i><b><a href="http://scientificforestry.com">The Scientific Forestry Service</a></b></i>, 2008		
	  <div style="border-bottom:1px solid #ddd;margin-bottom:5px;"></div>
	</p>
	<p>
		The Scientific Forestry Service is an audio label releasing long-form natural soundscapes, documenting the Earth's biophony, and exposing the musical structures of non-human organisms using the acoustic channel.
	</p>

	<p>
	  <img align="left" alt="Scify002" class="music_image" src="/images/scify002.jpg?1237664806" width="150" />
		<i><b><a href="http://scientificforestry.com/releases/SCIFY002" target="_blank">The Beaver Ponds in Clanton</a></b></i>, SCIFY002<br><br>
	  Its early June in Clanton, Alabama. Huge choruses of frogs and toads soak the early evening with syncopation against the backdrop of cicadas and evening songbirds.  	
	</p>
  <br />

	<p>
	  <img align="left" alt="Scify001" src="/images/scify001.jpg?1237664603"  class="music_image"  width="150" />
    <i><b><a href="http://scientificforestry.com/releases/SCIFY001" target="_blank">American Toad Chorus</a></b></i>, SCIFY001
    <br/>			<br/>
	  American Toads harmonize around a small pond in Ithaca, New York. The chorus is joined by Green Frogs and Spring Peepers. Pickerels are hiding in the reeds
	</p>
